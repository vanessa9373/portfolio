About Me

Turning business requirements into production-grade cloud infrastructure

I build cloud infrastructure that doesn't break at 2 AM. As a Solutions Architect, Cloud Engineer, DevOps Engineer, and Site Reliability Engineer, I design and operate multi-cloud environments across AWS, Azure, and GCP that are secure, cost-efficient, observable, and self-healing. I specialize in turning complex business requirements into resilient, production-grade infrastructure, from reusable Terraform modules and Kubernetes platforms to fully automated CI/CD pipelines and zero-downtime deployment strategies.

My engineering philosophy is simple: the best way to demonstrate skill is to ship. I've built 18 production-grade infrastructure labs, an 18-lab Kubernetes Mastery Guide spanning beginner to expert, and a 40-module multi-cloud Terraform library - all open source on GitHub. Every project reflects how I work: methodical, well-documented, and built to scale.

On the AWS side, I architect and automate across the full stack: EC2, EKS, ECS, Lambda, RDS, S3, CloudFront, IAM, VPC, CloudWatch, and beyond, with a strong emphasis on least-privilege security, cost governance, and infrastructure as code. I design pipelines using AWS CodePipeline, GitHub Actions, and ArgoCD, and build observability stacks with CloudWatch, Prometheus, and Grafana that give teams visibility before incidents become outages.

As an SRE, I treat reliability as a feature, defining SLOs, automating runbooks, building self-healing systems, and eliminating toil through platform automation. As a Cloud Engineer, I translate architecture blueprints into repeatable, auditable infrastructure. As a DevOps Engineer, I own the full delivery lifecycle - from commit to production, with security and compliance baked in, not bolted on.

I'm completing a B.S. in Computer Science at Western Governors University (May 2026) and hold an AWS Solutions Architect Associate and Linux Essentials certification, with the CKA (Certified Kubernetes Administrator) in progress. My earlier B.S. in Business Management gives me something many engineers lack: the ability to speak fluently with both engineering teams and business stakeholders, translating uptime requirements into SLO budgets, cloud costs into ROI, and technical risk into language executives actually act on.
